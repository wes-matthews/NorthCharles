North Charles Institute for the Addictions


North Charles Institute for the Addictions (NCIA) is a Department of Public Health, licensed and CARF accredited opioid treatment program. NCIA was one of the first methadone treatment programs in Massachusetts and remains a local and national leader in methadone treatment. The program offers a full range of addiction treatment services including daily dosing and toxicology screening at our Cambridge Hospital site, and individual, group and family counseling, case management, medical and on-site psychopharmacology services at our 54 Washburn Ave, Cambridge location. Our mission is to provide the highest quality drug treatment services in a safe, respectful, supportive environment. The guiding principles below are the hallmark of our services.


GUIDING PRINCIPLES:

  • Methadone maintenance treatment can assist clients in recovery from opiate addiction and other substances of abuse and provide the stability to work on individualized treatment goals.
  • An improved quality of life is achieved through recovering from addictive behavior, improving interpersonal relationships, developing physical, emotional and spiritual health, creating a meaningful support network, and developing vocational and life management skills.
  • Relapse can be part of the recovery process, and we will actively assist clients in all stages of recovery to minimize the harm that substance abuse causes in life.
